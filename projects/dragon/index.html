Exploration
of the
Heighway Dragon

The Heighway dragon is a member of a family of selfsimilar fractal curves. An approximation can be produced by taking a strip of paper and iteratively folding it in half to the right until physically impossible and subsequently unfolding and straighening each bend to 90°. The unfolded sequence of left and right turns is known as the regular paperfolding sequence.


The sequence represented with a left turn as 1 and a right turn as 0 with the first-order curve denoted 1, can be constructed from the process of appending a 1 and then repeating the preceding digits with its middle digit complemented. For example, the secondorder curve is generated as follows: (1)1 → (1)1(0) → 110, and the third as (110)1 → (110)1(100) → 1101100.

My goal was to explore image based derivation of patterns in the dragon curve through mathematical functions mapping current iteration to pixel RGB. To begin discovery I wrote code that would generate mappings of varied complexity which I would then sift through to identify notable outputs. Over the hours of generating batches of curves I would refine expression generation to yeild different output in hopes of increasing the odds of finding new mapping possiblities.

Here I present a selection of mapping functions that I found unique and/or aesthetically pleasing.
